'Tony Blair accused the Conservatives of being "ludicrously irresponsible'' and called their proposed budget “a recipe for disaster”.The Prime Minister made his comments during a speech at the annual conference of the Labour Party, where he argued that the Tories were setting themselves up for an electoral catastrophe if elected next month. He also warned that their austerity measures could lead to further damage to the economy.Tory leader Michael Howard defended the plan by claiming it was necessary to reduce government debt. However, he failed to acknowledge the fact that such drastic cuts could have serious consequences for public services.Shadow Home Secretary David Davis criticised the proposal as ‘dangerous’ and suggested that it lacked any real vision or direction.'
