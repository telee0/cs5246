Government Urges Councils to Find More Sites for Travellers Amid Unauthorised Encampments
In response to ongoing disputes over unauthorised traveller encampments, government ministers are instructing councils to identify additional sites for travellers to settle. This directive comes as tensions escalate over a series of unauthorized encampments that have sparked controversy nationwide. Additionally, councils will be granted enhanced authority to swiftly address illegal settlements established by Gypsy communities on rural properties. The government is taking proactive measures to address the issue of unauthorised encampments and ensure that all parties involved are adhering to the law. By encouraging councils to find suitable sites for travellers and empowering them to take action against illegal settlements, the government aims to promote harmony and compliance within these communities.
