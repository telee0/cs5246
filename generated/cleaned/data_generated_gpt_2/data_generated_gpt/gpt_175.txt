Former Conservative leader William Hague rules out standing for leadership again
William Hague, the former Conservative leader, has announced that he will not be seeking to lead the party again in the future. This decision comes as he has given up his ambition of becoming prime minister. At the age of 43, Mr Hague has stated that he believes a life dominated by politics would be too mundane and unfulfilling for him. This revelation was made in an interview with the Daily Telegraph, where he expressed his reasons for stepping back from the political spotlight. With this decision, Mr Hague has effectively ruled out the possibility of a return to leadership within the Conservative Party.
