Scottish Environment Agency warns of dire consequences of climate change
During a recent meeting with a committee of MSPs, the Scottish Environment Protection Agency issued a dire warning about the potential consequences of unchecked climate change. According to experts giving evidence to the Scottish Parliament's environment committee, if drastic action is not taken soon, the effects of climate change could spiral out of control within just a few decades. The agency highlighted the urgent need for measures to mitigate the impact of climate change, emphasizing the importance of swift and decisive action. It is clear that the stakes are high, and the time to act is now.
