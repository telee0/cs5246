UK Chancellor Gordon Brown pledges £960m to boost vaccinations and tackle child mortality rates
The UK Chancellor, Gordon Brown, has announced a £960m ($1.8bn) investment over 15 years in an international scheme aimed at improving vaccination and immunisation programs. In a passionate speech, Brown emphasized the importance of taking action to achieve the goals set out in the 2000 Millennium Declaration, which include halving global poverty and reducing child mortality rates. This significant financial commitment demonstrates the UK's dedication to improving global health outcomes and addressing key challenges in the fight against poverty. Brown's pledge comes at a crucial time when global cooperation and investment in healthcare are more important than ever before.
