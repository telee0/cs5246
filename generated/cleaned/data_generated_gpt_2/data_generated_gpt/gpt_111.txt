Gordon Brown: The Longest Serving Chancellor of Modern Times
Despite not yet achieving his ultimate goal of becoming the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom and residing at 10 Downing Street, Gordon Brown can take pride in the fact that he has achieved the title of the longest serving chancellor of modern times. Surpassing the previous record holder, David Lloyd George, who served for seven years and 43 days between 1908 and 1915, Brown reached this milestone in June of last year. As he prepares to deliver his ninth budget, Brown's legacy as chancellor is solidified, showcasing his expertise and longevity in the world of politics.
