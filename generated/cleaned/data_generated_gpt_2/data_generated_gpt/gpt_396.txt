Trial for Manslaughter in Hatfield Rail Crash: Engineering Firm and Managers to Face Proceedings
Engineering firm Balfour Beatty and five railway managers are set to stand trial for manslaughter in connection with the tragic Hatfield rail crash of 2000. The crash, which resulted in the deaths of four individuals, occurred when a section of rail fractured causing a high speed train to derail. This incident marked a significant turning point in the discussion surrounding railway safety in the UK. The trial is a crucial step towards bringing accountability and justice for the victims and their families. The events of the Hatfield rail crash shook the nation and raised serious concerns about the maintenance and safety protocols within the railway industry.
